CS 170—Week 11

Exercises & Assignments


Here are some exercises from this week's lessons that will give you some practice writing your own classes.

  1. A Simple Class—PongScore: In your textbook, you completed the PongBall class for a game of Pong. Follow those same steps on your own, and complete the classes, PongScore.

    Check your work with the Check program. If you get stuck, look on the solutions page.

  2. A More Complex Class—PongPaddle: In your textbook, you completed the PongBall class for a game of Pong. Follow those same steps on your own, and complete the classes, PongPaddle.

    Check your work with the Check program. If you get stuck, look on the solutions page.

  3. A Class from Scratch—MyEmployee: This week you learned how to create classes. Try it out. Implement the class MyEmployee. An employee has a name (a string) and a salary (a double). Provide the following constructor and methods:

    public MyEmployee(String name, double initialSalary)
    public String getName()
    public double getSalary()
    public void raiseSalary(double byPercent)

    These methods return the name and salary, and raise the employee's salary by a certain percentage. Here's a sample usage:

    MyEmployee steve = new MyEmployee("Gilbert, Steve", 95000.0);
    steve.raiseSalary(10); // gimme a 10% raise

    Check your work with the Check program. If you get stuck, look on the solutions page.
