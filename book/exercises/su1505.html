CS 170—Chapter 5

Exercises and Assignments


The big idea in this chapter is writing your own procedures and functions. Here's the list of things you should do:

  1. Drawing Shapes: Read Section 5.1—Writing Procedures, where you'll learn how to take a large, monolithic program, and decompose it into smaller, reusable procedures. Then, tackle this problem which asks you to take an ASCII-Art drawing, similar to those you write for PQ01, and use functional decomposition to break it into procedures. The goal of this assignment is to use procedures to eliminate redundancy.
  2. Your First Functions: Earlier in the semester, you wrote an IPO program, Greeter, that asked your user for their name, and then crafted a greeting from the input they supplied. In Section 5.3—Writing Functions, you'll learn how to create "mini-IPO" programs, called functions. These two hands-on exercises will give you some step-by-step tutoring writing your own functions.
