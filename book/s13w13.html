Classes & Testing


Welcome to Chapter 13. In the last chapter you learned to create your own user-defined types. We'll start this chapter by covering a few more class-design topics: scope, static members, factory and overloaded methods. Then, we'll turn our attention to testing and bugs.

Most programs contain errors of one kind or another. Your debugger will help you fix the errors in your code, but only if you know they exist. Finding your bugs and putting them on display is the job of software testing.

In this chapter you'll:

  • add members that are shared among all instances of your class.
  • apply the different rules for access and scope.
  • learn to create factory and overloaded methods.
  • make your classes more robust using assertions.
  • add error-checking to your methods with exceptions.
  • respond to runtime errors by catching and fixing them.
  • test your programs using the JUnit testing framework.
