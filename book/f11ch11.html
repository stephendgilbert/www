User-Defined Types


Welcome to Chapter 11. For the last few weeks you've worked with graphical objects, using classes in the AWT, Swing, and ACM libraries. Where, though, do such classes come from?

The objects you've been using are not built into the Java language; instead, they were designed by programmers to meet a specific need. In this chapter, you'll learn how to define your own classes, rather than just using objects that others have designed.

Learning how to define your own types is really at the heart of Object-Oriented Programming.

In this chapter you'll:

  • create and define the fields that store your objects' state.
  • write instance methods that provide your objects with behavior.
  • learn how to use encapsulation to protect your objects' data.
  • write constructors to initialize your new objects.
  • apply your class-creation skills to the game of Pong
