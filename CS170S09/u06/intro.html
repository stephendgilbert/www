Welcome to Unit 6 This week you'll continue working with methods and loops, but you'll also learn how to work with collections of values: groups of related objects that can be treated as single unit, or that can be processed with loops to automate various kinds of calculations.

This week you'll learn:

  • what array variables are, and why they're useful.
  • how to define and initialize array variables.
  • how to store and retrieve values using an array.
  • how to use arrays with methods and loops.

To the right are the online course materials for this unit. Along with the online lessons, you should read the first portion of Chapter 7 in your textbook.

In addition to the reading, you'll need to complete the Unit 6 quiz, and have your homework assignments graded by 11:55 pm on Sunday Tuesday, .

Along with the online lessons, you'll also find a sample programming exam along with an annotated solution. Set aside a few hours to try the exam and then walk through the solution.

The first midterm exam will take place during the regular class period next week (March 23 and March 24). The exam will consist of two parts:

  • a 75-question, closed-book, closed-notes short-answer/multiple-choice exam with questions from the online lectures (Units 1-65), the in-class lab periods and Chapters 1,4,5,6 and 7 in your textbook.
  • a programming problem you will have to solve at your computer that will be graded using Web-Cat. You may use your textbook, the JavaDocs, and hand-written crib-notes during the programming exam. The programming exam will not have the array problem. There will be four problems only.
