Welcome to Unit 9. For the last few weeks you've worked with graphical objects, using classes in the AWT, Swing, and ACM libraries. Where, though, do such classes come from?

The objects you've been using are not built into the Java language; instead, they were designed by programmers to meet a specific need. In this unit, you'll learn how to define your own classes, rather than just using objects that others have designed.

Learning how to define your own types is really at the heart of Object-Oriented Programming. In this unit you'll:

  • create and define the fields that store your objects' state.
  • write methods that provide your objects with behavior.
  • learn how to use encapsulation to protect your objects' data.
  • write constructors to initialize your new objects.
  • add members that are shared among all instances of your class.
  • apply the different rules for access and scope.
