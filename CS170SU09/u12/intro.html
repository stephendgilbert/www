Welcome to Unit 12. In this final unit, you'll continue working with arrays and other classes. You'll also study the fundamental concepts of abstract classes, interfaces, and exceptions.

In this unit you'll learn:

  • how to create abstract classes and implement interfaces.
  • how to use Java's event-handling mechanism.
  • how to create and process multi-dimensional arrays
  • how to use try-catch-finally to process runtime errors.

To the right are the online course materials for this unit. Along with this material, there is a practice programming problem for the final exam. In your textbook, you'll want to finish up Chapters 11, 15 and 18.

There are is one final homework assignment this week, but no quiz. The sample multiple-choice exam will include some questions from this unit. You should also work on the practial programming Sample Final exam. The material in these lessons will be on the Final exam.

By next Monday you should:

  • read each of the online lessons.
  • take the practical programming sample exam.
  • take the sample multiple-choice exam.
  • study hard!
