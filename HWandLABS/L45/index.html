Lab 45

Digital Pictures


For our first Lab this week, you're going to work with your own digital picture, scaling it and drawing on it. If you forgot to bring a picture (and you can't download one from Facebook, or take one with your phone), I'll take one for you.

L45: Your Small Picture

In class you worked with the Picture class. For this lab you're going to create a small, framed version of your picture, with your name on it. Here are the instructions, and here's an example with one of my pictures.

  1. Copy your digital picture into DrJava's Chapter09 folder. Rename the picture so that it includes both your first and last name, and so that it's easy to enter into your source code.
  2. Open MySmallPicture.java in the Chapter09 folder.
  3. Create a Picture object using your digital picture. Don't use the FileChooser class; just use the name of the file (with no path) to create your picture. Leave the code I've supplied to calculate the full path to the picture.
  4. Scale the picture so that the finished size is 275 pixels wide and 200 pixels high. To see what that is in percentages you'll have to use the getWidth() and getHeight() methods on the original picture.

    Note: this requires you to do some calculations.

  5. Use createGraphics() to get a pen to paint with on the new, smaller picture. This pen will be type Graphics2D.
  6. Uncomment the line that smoothes the drawing.
  7. Draw a square frame around your picture. This may take repeated drawRect() or some creative fillRect statements.
  8. Draw your name at the bottom of your portrait, in the center.
  9. Save the picture in the same folder, using the same name, but with the addition of "-smaller". Modify the original fileName variable, and use the write() method.

Read the lecture slides (on your desktop) for hints on the code you'll need to complete this assignment.

Once you have created the program, compile it and run it. Only when it looks like you want, should you try to Check it. The Check program only grades the basic syntax and structure of your program. When you check your program, I'll take your smaller picture and put it on the monitor at the front of the room. Once your picture appears there, and you are happy with it, you can close DrJava and go home.

Notice that the Check program gives you 100%, even if you don't do anything. I will check the actual picture produced, and take points off (up to 100%), if it doesn't meet the requirements. Check your work on the screen!
