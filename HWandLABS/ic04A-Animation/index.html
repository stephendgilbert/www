Animation—Pac Man


In class, you learned how to apply loops to the process of animation. Let's put that to work, (and, get a little more practice building graphical applications), by writing an animated version of the arcade-game PacMan character.

Animating PacMan

The title character in the PacMan series of arcade games is pretty easy to draw using a yellow-filled GArc. Using the starter code from your Week04 folder (which just creates the window the correct size), add a PacMan character at the far left side of the screen. Use the standard animation skeleton to move him to the right side, where he'll reverse course and start chomping his way back to the left.

Step 1: Get the PacMan Displayed

To get started, you'll need to create some instance variables.

  • You'll need a GArc to represent the PacMan character.
  • You'll need a double variable, dx to represent how far the PacMan moves during each frame.
  • You'll need two double variables, startAngle and sweepAngle to tell you where to start the "mouth", and how far it should be opened or closed.
  • You'll need a double variable mx that represents how much the mouth should open or close on each frame of the animation.

Finally, you'll need a constant for MOUTH set to 90.0. This is the widest that the mouth can get before you need to change directions.

Inside your init() method, initialize your PacMan variable by creating a GArc object, (make it about half or three-quarters the height of the application). Initialize startAngle to 45, sweepAngle to 360 - MOUTH, and use those for the last two arguments to your GArc constructor.

Set the fill color to yellow, and make sure your GArc is displayed as filled. Then, add it to the left side of your application, in the vertical center of the app.

Step 2: Moving the Pac Man

To move the PacMan, add the standard animation skeleton to the run() method. Inside each frame:

  • Move the PacMan by dx pixels. You do this by calling the move() method, and passing 0 for the y parameter (since we don't want to move vertically.)
  • After moving, call the PacMan's getX() method to find out where it is. If x is less than 0, or, if x plus the width of your PacMan is greater than the APPLICATION_WIDTH, then reverse the sign of dx, and move() the PacMan once again.

Check to make sure the PacMan moves across the screen and bounces back when it gets to either side. Don't go on until you get this part working.

Step 3: Animating the Mouth

To animate the mouth, at each frame, subtract mx / 2 from the start angle, and add mx to the sweep angle. When the sweep angle goes to zero, or, when the sweep angle gets larger than 360 - MOUTH, then change the sign of the "change" variable, mx.

Even though the PacMan bounces correctly, when he switches direction and starts moving right-to-left, his mouth is still facing right-ward. In other words, he isn't "chomping" in the same direction that he's traveling. To change the direction of the PacMan, just add 180 to startAngle when you change the sign of dx.

Finally, even though you are changing the values of startAngle and sweepAngle, it's NOT changing the PacMan object. At the end of each frame (inside your loop, before you call pause() call the GArc methods setStartAngle() and setSweepAngle().

Once you have the program working like you want it, should you try to Check it. The Check program grades the basic syntax and structure of your program. When you check your program, I'll take a picture of your PacMan put it on the monitor at the front of the room. Once your picture appears there, and you are happy with it, you can close DrJava and go home.
