Objects, Fonts and Colors


For our final Lab this week, we're going to practice with creating objects and sending messages. To do that, I'm going to have you work with ahother Java applet, adding fonts and colors.

L15: Business Card

Now that you know how to change colors and how to use different fonts, complete the applet named BusinessCard that you'll find in the Chapter03 folder. Here are the requirements.

  • Add your name, a title and your email address.
  • The data should fit on a standard 3.5" x 2" business card. (I've added a method to automatically size the window when run in DrJava.)
  • Use at least two different fonts and two different colors.
  • Change the background color in the init() method, not in the paint() method.

Once you have created the program, compile it and run it. Only when it looks like you want, should you try to Check it.

The Check program only grades the basic syntax and structure of your applet. When you check your program, I'll take a picture and put it on the monitor at the front of the room. Once your picture appears there, and you are happy with it, you can close DrJava and go home.

Notice that the Check program gives you 100%, even if you don't do anything. I will check the actual applet produced, and take points off (up to 100%), if it doesn't meet the requirements. Check your work on the screen!
