Week 6—September 29 to October 5
Graphics and Methods

Welcome to CS 170 Week 6. This week, we're going to focus on methods and graphics. There are no online lectures this week, so you can study for the first midterm exam which will cover Chapters 1-4 in your text.

During in-class lecture this week you'll learn:

  • how to create convert between numeric and character types
  • how to format numeric and string output
  • how to apply methods and functional decomposition
  • how to use the drawing methods in the Graphics class
  • how to create and use Color objects

To the right is the link to the handouts from this week's in-class lecture. You'll also find a sample programming exam along with an annotated solution. Set aside a few hours to try the exam and then walk through the solution.

There is only one homework exercise this week, but you won't need to submit it until Noon on Monday, October 13, along with the online assignments for Week 7.

The first midterm exam will take place during the regular class period next week. The exam will consist of two parts:

  • a 50-question, closed-book, closed-notes short-answer/multiple-choice exam with questions from the lectures and Chapters 1-4 in your textbook
  • a programming problem you will have to solve at your computer that will be graded using Web-Cat. You may use your textbook, the JavaDocs, and hand-written notes during the programming exam.

You will need to bring your Web-Cat password to complete the programming exam.

See you online!
--Steve
