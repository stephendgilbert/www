Week 7— October 6 to October 12
Graphics & Decisions

Welcome to CS 170 Week 7. This week we'll continue with a "graphics interlude", working on our Artist applet, adding variables and fonts. Then, you'll switch gears and learn how to use the object-oriented ACM graphics classes. Finally, we'll finish up meeting the boolean primitive type and the if statement.

This week you'll learn:

  • how to use Java's Font and measuring classes
  • how to use the ACM GraphicsProgram class
  • how to work with GLabel and GImage objects
  • how to write code to respond to mouse events
  • how to use boolean variables and methods
  • how to make decisions with the if statement

To the right are links to the online course materials for this week. There are no handouts for the in-class lecture, since we had the first midterm exam instead. Along with the lessons here, you should also start reading Chapter 5 in your textbook. We'll continue to cover Chapter 5 next week. The Chapter 5 quiz will be week-after-next.

In addition to the reading, you'll need to complete the lab and homework exercises and submit them by Noon on Monday, October 13 Tuesday, October 14.

By then you should:

  • listen to each of the online lessons.
  • complete and submit the ic10 online lab exercises.
  • complete and submit the Week 6 and 7 homework assignments.

The lessons this week again include extensive hands-on lab exercises. Remember to budget at about twice the running time of the lectures to complete them. Remember also that the Week 6 homework assignment (your self portrait) will be due along with the Week 7 assignments.

See you online!
--Steve
