Week 9— October 20 to October 26
Eclipse and Loops

Welcome to CS 170 Week 9. This week you'll teach your objects to work without constant supervision. Once you teach them the skill of iteration, you can turn them loose with a set of instructions, and not come back until the job is done. In addition, you'll start using a new Java development environment, the Eclipse IDE.

This week you'll learn:

  • use the Eclipse Integrated Development Environment.
  • to write counted loops with for and while.
  • to write indefinite loops that handle varied inputs.
  • to use Java's jump statements in different loops./li>
  • to use iterators to read a text file.
  • learn a strategy for writing loops.

To the right are links to the in-class lecture slides as well as the online course materials for this week. Along with the lessons here, you should also finish reading Chapter 6 in your textbook. The Chapter 6 quiz will be given during class next week.

In addition to the reading, you'll need to complete the lab and homework exercises and submit them by Noon on Monday, October 27 Tuesday, October 28.

By then you should:

  • listen to each of the online lessons.
  • complete and submit the ic14 online lab exercises.
  • complete and submit the Week 9 homework assignments.

The Eclipse@Home lecture is optional if you intend to do all of your work in the Computing Center. As before, the other lessons this week again include extensive hands-on lab exercises. Remember to budget at about twice the running time of the lectures to complete them.

See you online!
--Steve
