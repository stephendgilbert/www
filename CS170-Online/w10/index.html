Week 10— October 27 to November 2
Enumerated Types & Random Numbers

Welcome to CS 170 Week 10. This week, we're going to continue working with Eclipse. We'll also learn how to use enumerated types and how to work with random numbers. There are no online lectures this week, so you can study for the second midterm exam which will cover Chapters 5 and 6 in your text.

During the on-campus lecture this week you'll learn:

  • how to configure Eclipse preferences.
  • how to use a template library to create new programs.
  • how to use Java Enums to create user-defined single-value types.
  • to generate and work with random numbers.

To the right is the link to the handouts from this week's on-campus lecture. You'll also find a sample programming exam along with an annotated solution. Set aside a few hours to try the exam and then walk through the solution.

There are only two homework exercises this week, but you won't need to submit them until Noon on Monday, November 17 Tuesday, November 18, along with the online assignments for Weeks 11 and 12.

The second midterm exam will take place during the regular class period next week. The exam will consist of two parts:

  • a 50-question, closed-book, closed-notes short-answer/multiple-choice exam with questions from the lectures and Chapters 5-6 in your textbook
  • a programming problem you will have to solve at your computer that will be graded using Web-Cat. You may use your textbook, the JavaDocs, and hand-written notes during the programming exam. The programming exam will use the Eclipse IDE.

You will need to bring your Web-Cat password to complete the programming exam.

See you online!
--Steve
