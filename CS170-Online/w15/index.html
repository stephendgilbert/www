Week 15— December 1 to December 7
Events and Exceptions

Welcome to CS 170 Week 15. This week we'll finish looking at selected topics in Chapters 9, 10 and 11 in your textbook. Remember that you won't be responsible for all of the material in these three chapters; just what we cover in lecture. This week we'll cover the topics of event and exception handling.

This week you'll learn:

  • how to implement Java event handling
  • how to respond to mouse events
  • how to respond to button clicks
  • how to create inner classes and use event adaptors
  • how to use catch runtime exceptions that occur in your code
  • how to throw exceptions to validate parameters

To the right is a links to the slides from the in-class lecture this week. There are no on-line lectures or homework exercises. Instead, I've provided two sample final exams you can use for studying. The multiple-choice exam is on Blackboard Vista and consists of the same sorts of questions you'll have on the actual final. After you take the multiple-choice exam, you can see your answers and your score, to help you prepare for the actual final. The sample programming final is similar to the actual final programming exam. In addition you might want to study some of the non-assigned problems in the Arrays2 section of the JavaBat practice site.

See you next week in class!
