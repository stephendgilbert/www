Weeks 11— November 3 to November 9
Introducing Arrays

Welcome to CS 170 Week 11. This week you'll continue working with methods and loops, but you'll also learn how to work with collections of values: groups of related objects that can be treated as single unit, or that can be processed with loops to automate various kinds of calculations.

This week you'll learn:

  • what array variables are, and why they're useful.
  • how to define and initialize array variables.
  • how to store and retrieve values using an array.
  • how to use arrays with methods and loops.

To the right are links to the online course materials for this week. There are no handouts for the in-class lecture since you'll take the second Midterm Exam during your on-campus meeting. You should start reading Chapter 7 in your textbook.

In addition to the reading, you'll need to complete the lab and homework exercises and submit them by Noon on Monday, November 17 Tuesday, November 18 (week after next).

By then you should:

  • listen to each of the online lessons.
  • complete and submit the ic16 online lab exercises.
  • complete and submit the Week 10, 11, and 12 homework assignments.

Remember that the Week 10 homework assignments will be due along with the Week 11 and 12 assignments. Because of the second Midterm and the Veterans' Day holiday, you'll have two weeks to complete these assignments.

See you online!
