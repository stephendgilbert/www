Week 5—September 22 to September 28
Fundamental Data Types

Welcome to CS 170 Week 5. This week, we're going to zoom in on Java's fundamental data types: the numbers, characters, and calculations that provide the supporting mechanics for classes and objects.

This week you'll learn:

  • how to create interactive, console-based programs
  • how to use Java's integer and real number types
  • how to combine operators and operands to make expressions
  • how the different side-effect operators work
  • how to use String and Math methods

To the right are links to the handouts from this week's in-class lecture as well as the online course materials for this week. Along with the lessons here, you should also read Chapter 4 in your textbook. We'll have a quiz on Chapter 4 during the on-campus class next week.

In addition to the reading, you'll need to complete the lab and homework exercises and submit them by Noon on Monday, September 29.

By then you should:

  • listen to each of the online lessons.
  • complete and submit the ic08 online lab exercises.
  • complete and submit the Week 5 homework assignments.

The lessons this week again include extensive hands-on lab exercises and the homework assignments require a lot of programming. Remember to budget at least twice the running time of the lectures this week, possibly more, to complete them. Make sure you leave time for the homework assignments as well.

See you online!
--Steve
