Week 12— November 10 to November 16
Class Design and Testing

Welcome to CS 170 Week 12. This week we're "skipping" ahead one chapter in your textbook, and looking at the topic of class design. We'll also cover the important topics of testing and debugging. Next week we'll come back and finish up Chapter 7.

This week and next, you'll learn:

  • how to write programs with static fields and methods
  • to explain and apply Java's different scope types
  • how to write overloaded methods
  • how to use the Eclipse debugger
  • how to write JUnit test programs

To the right are links to the online course materials for this week. There are no handouts for the in-class lecture because of the Veterans' Day holiday. Those of you in the Monday and Wednesday classes will have a lab period where I'll be available to help you with your Nim and Rabbit Hunt assignments. Along with the online lessons here, you should also read Chapter 8 in your textbook. The Chapter 8 quiz will be next week.

In addition to the reading, you'll need to complete the lab and homework exercises and submit them by Noon on Monday, November 17 Tuesday, November 18. Note that the assignment(s) from Week 10 and 11 are also due at that time.

By then you should:

  • listen to each of the online lessons.
  • complete and submit the ic17 online lab exercises.
  • complete and submit the Week 10, 11, and 12 homework assignments.

The lessons this week again include extensive hands-on lab exercises. Remember to budget at about twice the running time of the lectures to complete them. Remember also that the Week 10 and 11 homework assignments will be due along with the Week 12 assignments.

See you online!
--Steve
