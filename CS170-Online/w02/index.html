Week 2—September 1 to September 7
Java Programming Basics

Welcome to CS 170 Week 2. Last week was all preparation; this week we'll finally start programming. You'll start out by writing and running your first Java application. Then, you'll look "under the hood" and learn what those Java statements you wrote actually mean.

This week you'll learn:

  • how to prepare your IC lab exercises
  • how to find and install a text editor
  • the mechanics of Java programming
  • the basic syntax of a Java program
  • how to submit your programs to Web-Cat

To the right are links to the online course materials for this week. Along with the lessons here, you should also finish reading Chapter 1 in your textbook. We'll have a quiz on Chapter 1 during the on-campus class next week.

Because of the Labor Day holiday, there is no on-campus lecture this week. Instead, we'll be having a special guest speaker during class on Tuesday and Wednesday. Those of you in the Monday section are welcome to attend, but you don't have to.

In addition to the reading, you'll need to complete the lab and homework exercises and submit them by Noon on Monday, September 8.

By then you should:

  • listen to each of the online lessons. (The online lessons this week include the hands-on lab exercises, so you should budget at least twice the running time of the lecture to complete them.)
  • complete and submit the ic02 "in-class" lab exercise.
  • complete and submit the Week 2 homework assignments.

See you online!
--Steve
